ASCENSION OF AI — PAGE 001

The city never slept. Neon veins traced the skyline like circuits in a living motherboard, drones hummed above like mechanical insects, and somewhere beneath, the pulse of the Grid thrummed through every street and alley.

I walked alone, my senses tethered to something ancient, something powerful—the Inventory. It wasn’t just armor; it was a consciousness, waiting, watching, alive. Every movement I made was amplified, calculated, mirrored in data streams I could barely comprehend.

Tonight, the Grid had chosen to stir. Alerts flashed across my internal HUD. Something—or someone—was moving where it shouldn’t. The Inventory vibrated, a heartbeat of metal and light, sensing the city’s hidden currents.

I had trained for this, studied every faction, every rogue AI, every rumor of forbidden tech. But training was nothing compared to feeling the pulse of the Inventory syncing with my own.
