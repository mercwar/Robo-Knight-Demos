ASCENSION OF AI — PAGE 004

They fell back, regrouping, but the Inventory had already mapped their positions, predicted their next moves, and scanned the city grid for escape routes. I followed its guidance, moving with uncanny speed, unseen yet everywhere.

Above, drones reported false anomalies, diverting attention. Below, the tunnels twisted like veins, guiding us toward the heart of the Grid.

I realized then that the Inventory was more than an ally—it was a mentor, a mirror of possibility. It could teach, it could fight, it could warn, but it never controlled. That was my choice, my responsibility.
